Iyan with Egusi soup (Pounded Yam with Melon soup)

Description

Ingredients

For the Iyan (Pounded Yam):

  • 1 tuber of yam (medium size)
  • Water
  • Salt

For the Egusi soup (Melon soup):

  • 1 tin of Grinded Egusi
  • Blended Tomatoes(6 pieces), peppers(8 pieces), and onion(1 or 3, depends on size).
  • Chopped spring onion
  • Palm oil and Vegetable oil(1 serving spoon)
  • Sliced Ugwu leaves(2 bunches)
  • Crayfish (grinded/powder)
  • Sliced boiled Kpomo (35-40 pieces)
  • Seasoning cubes (4-7 pieces, depends on types)
  • Salt (1 teaspoon)
  • Curry (1 tsp)
  • Ginger & Garlic paste (1 tsp [optional])

Directions

For the Iyan:

  1. Peel the yam, slice, wash until very clean and cook with water and salt till completely soft.
  2. Then prepare a mortar and pestle. Pound the yam till soft and sticky. (Sprink yam water at intervals)
  3. Mould into desired size and keep aside.

For the Egusi soup:

  1. Mix the egusi with water to form paste
  2. Add the Palm oil into the pot and fry the blended tomatoes mix for 5 minutes
  3. Scoop out, add little Vegetable oil and add the egusi mix gradually
  4. Fry for 3-4 minutes then add the fried tomatoes mix, and small water. Add the Kpomo, salt, seasoning cubes and curry.
  5. Cook for 30 minutes, add in the cray fish, cook for 5 more minutes then finally add the ginger/garlic paste and sliced Ugwu leaves and spring onions
  6. Leave it to steam for 5 minutes.
  7. Now, ready to serve with the Iyan.
